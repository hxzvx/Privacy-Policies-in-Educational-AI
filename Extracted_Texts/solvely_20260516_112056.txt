Personal, Sensitive and Non-Personal Information We Collect and How We Use and Disclose It
We gather information about you in several ways: directly from your interactions with our Services, automatically through cookies and similar technologies when you visit our Services, and occasionally from third-party sources. Some of the data we collect may be considered " personal data " (or " personal information ") and, in certain cases, may be deemed " sensitive, " under relevant laws. We also recognize that certain information, such as IP addresses and cookie identifiers, may be treated as "personal data" where required by applicable regulations.
In some instances, we may de-identify or pseudonymize your personal data to render it non-personal, either by aggregating it with data from other individuals or by applying techniques such as hashing to remove characteristics that could identify you. We will handle such de-identified or pseudonymized data as non-personal data, in line with the limits set by applicable laws. We manage and utilize de-identified data without attempting to re-identify it, except where allowed by law, such as to verify the effectiveness of our de-identification processes. If we combine non-personal data with personal data, we will treat the combined information as personal data under this Privacy Policy. We may share non-personal data with third parties for various purposes at our discretion, including for advertising, research and marketing, as permitted by law.

A. Data We Collect Automatically
a. Device Data/Information.
We, along with our service providers, business partners, and other third parties, may automatically gather and store specific information when you access or engage with the Services (" Device Data/Information "). Details about your device, such as the model, operating system version, device date and time, and mobile network information;

Information about how you interact with the Services, including search queries, pages visited, content viewed, and your preferences;
Device Data/Information may be collected through various technologies, including:
Cookies and Local Storage.
Cookies and local storage are small text files placed on your browser or device when you access the Services. Some web browsers and browser extensions may offer additional methods for storing local data. We utilize cookies, including first-party cookies, and similar technologies to enhance the security of your use of the Services, analyze traffic patterns, evaluate the effectiveness of our customer communications, maintain the integrity of the Services, gather crash analytics and other maintenance-related data, manage and assess the performance of advertisements displayed on or delivered through the Services, and personalize your experience, such as recognizing you upon your return. Most browsers allow you to disable, decline, or delete cookies and local storage; refer to your browser's settings for more details. However, disabling cookies may impact your ability to access certain features of the Services. For additional information on cookies, please visit https://www.aboutcookies.org.
Web Beacons.
We may use small graphic images or other types of web programming code (commonly referred to as web beacons, pixel tags, 1x1 GIFs, or clear GIFs) within our web pages and email communications. These technologies serve various purposes, such as tracking the number of visitors to the Services, observing how users navigate through the Services, measuring the open rates of our email communications, and monitoring how often specific products are viewed. Information gathered through these technologies may include the date and time you first open our email and whether you interact with any links within the email.
Embedded Scripts.
Embedded scripts are programming codes integrated into the Services, intended to gather data on your interactions, such as the links you select. These scripts are temporarily activated by our server, a third-party service provider, or a business partner while you are using or interacting with the Services. Once you leave or disconnect from the Services, the code is either deactivated or removed.
Software Development Kits (SDKs) . SDKs are software bundles that provide a collection of tools designed to assist in building applications and integrating new features in existing apps. These tools enable the creation and operation of features that gather data from your device.
Data Collection from Mobile Devices . We may utilize embedded scripts and SDK tools to gather data from mobile devices, including hardware models, operating system versions, and unique identification numbers assigned to your device (such as the ID for Advertising (IDFA) on Apple devices and the Advertising ID on Android devices), mobile network details, and your browser behavior. Additionally, we may employ various other technologies that capture similar information for purposes such as security, fraud detection, and system maintenance.
